course Overview

Chemistry is a natural science that studies matter, its composition, structure, properties, and the energy that accompanies changes in matter. Chemistry also studies the interactions and transformations of matter to form materials that exist in everyday life.

Chemistry has an important role in various fields, such as health, agriculture, industry, biology, archeology and law. Some examples of the benefits of chemistry in everyday life are: Accessing electricity with the help of batteries, Communicating with touch screen cellphones, Preserving food, Making medicines to treat various diseases.

The word chemistry comes from Arabic, namely Al-Kimiya which means change in matter. Chemistry was first introduced by an Arab scientist named Jahir Ibnu Hayyan in 700 AD.

Branches of chemistry include: organic chemistry, inorganic chemistry, analytical chemistry, physical chemistry, biochemistry.

What you'll learn

Substances and Substances: Study various types of substances, both elements and compounds, and their characteristics.

Chemical Reactions: Investigates how substances change into other substances through reaction processes, including stoichiometry and reaction mechanisms.

Physical and Chemical Properties: Examines properties that can be observed and measured, such as boiling point, melting point, density, and reactivity.

Organic and Inorganic Chemistry: Organic chemistry focuses on carbon compounds, while inorganic chemistry studies compounds that do not contain carbon.

Physical Chemistry: Combines the principles of physics with chemistry to understand the energetic and kinetic aspects of reactions.

Analytical Chemistry: Techniques and methods used to analyze the composition of substances.
