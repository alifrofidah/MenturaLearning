course Overview

Physics is a branch of science that studies the properties and behavior of matter and energy, as well as the interactions between the two. Physics seeks to understand natural phenomena through observations, experiments, and mathematical models. Physics has wide applications, from everyday technology to scientific research, and is the basis for many other scientific disciplines, such as engineering, astronomy and biology.

What you'll learn

Matter: Study of the basic substances and structures that make up the universe.

Energy: Includes various forms of energy, such as kinetic, potential, thermal, and electrical energy.

Force and Motion: Explore the laws that govern the movement of objects and the influence of force on that movement

Waves: Investigates the properties of waves, including sound, light, and electromagnetic waves.

Natural Laws: Formulate the laws that govern physical phenomena, such as Newton's laws, the laws of thermodynamics, and the laws of electromagnetism.
